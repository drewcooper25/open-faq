BIG OPEN Survey FAQs

Frequently asked questions regarding our Big OPEN Survey

What is the purpose of your study?

We want to find out if the use of do-it-yourself artificial pancreas systems (DIYAPS; open source loop systems) has an influence on the health and well-being of people with diabetes. Additionally, we want to examine the challenges associated with building DIYAPS, and why some people choose not to use DIYAPS.

How can I support the work of the OPEN team?

  • Participate in our study on DIYAPS by completing the OPEN survey.
  • Join Open Humans and link your account to the OPEN project in order to enable us to message you anonymously, e.g. for follow-up studies.
  • Upload your CGM data to Open Humans and allow us to use them to deepen our research.
  • Send a participation link to your partner.
  • Sign-in to our study, give permission to your teenage child (13-17 years) to fill out some questionnaires and send them a participation link.
  • Tell people about our project - in person or online.
  • Share the URL to our website (link to according website page) with friends who might be interested, e.g. via e-mail.

I don’t live in Europe. Can I still participate in your study?

Of course! EU-funded doesn’t mean that we are restricted to Europe. This is a global study — the more people participate, the better it will be!

I am using a closed-loop system, but it is not DIY/open source. I am not using any loop/do-it-yourself artificial pancreas system (DIYAPS). Can I participate in your study?

Yes! We are also looking for participants who are looping with a commercial loop and for participants who are not looping at all. Read more here: Who can participate…

Who can participate in your study?

We are looking for:

  • users of open source loop / do-it-yourself artificial pancreas systems (DIYAPS): Adults ( 18+ years) and Teenagers (13-17 years; with parental permission)
  • partners of DIYAPS users and of people with diabetes who are not using DIYAPS
  • parents/caregivers of children (2-17 years) who use DIYAPS
  • parents/caregivers of children (2-17 years) who are not using DIYAPS
  • people with diabetes (18+ years) who are interested in DIYAPS but do not use such systems
  • users of commercial closed-loop systems (18+ years)

My child is younger than 2 years and I would like to participate on their behalf.

We are very sorry but it is not possible to participate on behalf of your child if they are younger than 2 years old. This is because certain questionnaires are not available for children this young.

I am using a DIYAPS in open-loop mode. Can I participate?

Yes! If you haven't closed the loop (yet), you can still participate in our study. During the questionnaire, you will get the opportunity to specify the system you are using.

Can I participate in your research study anonymously or do I have to give my name?

We want you to participate anonymously. If you have read the information sheet and decided to take part in our survey, you will first be given a participant ID which guarantees your anonymity in the survey.

In case you decide to also join Open Humans and link your account to us, we can contact you anonymously without accessing any of your personal information on Open Humans.

If you have uploaded or decide to now upload your CGM device data to Open Humans and think this is a good opportunity to share it or some of it from there with us, we will protect your data…GDPR …ethics...

How much time will it take to complete the survey?

The time needed to complete the survey depends on the type of participant (e.g. less time for teenagers and partners). We have several, mostly very short questionnaires for you, but all in all it takes approximately 10 to 20 or 20 to 30 minutes. You can always have a break and come back to where you left off in the questionnaires. For this you will need your participant ID.

What is a participant ID?

The protection of your identity and data is very important to us. Our study is under the General Data Protection Guidelines (GDPR) mandated by the EU, ensuring maximum protection and personal control for participants over their information. As such, in order to keep you anonymous we do not ask for your real name, but generate a Participant ID.

We ask you to write down, take a picture or email your Participant ID to yourself and keep it safe. You will need it later.

This survey seems complex. Can you please list exactly what I’m supposed to do as a participant?

  1. If you belong to our target group (see: Who can participate), we kindly invite you to complete the survey we have prepared in line with the Ethics Commission of the UCD….

  3. If you are interested in helping us take our research to the next level by combining your answers from the survey questionnaires with your health-related data, you can also “fill” your account on Open Humans with your CGM data and provide us access (see How-to below).

Uploading data to Open Humans can be done in several ways. This will be thoroughly explained at the survey and here (link) on our website.
